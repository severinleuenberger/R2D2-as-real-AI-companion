THE PROBLEM
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Your audio wire is connected to Jetson J511 Pin 9, which is the LEFT channel.
But your speaker is on the RIGHT channel outputs of the PAM8403 amplifier.

This is a CHANNEL MISMATCH:
  • You're sending LEFT audio to a RIGHT speaker
  • No audio comes out → No beep heard


THE SOLUTION
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Move your audio wire from J511 Pin 9 to J511 Pin 5

Currently:    J511 Pin 9 (HPO_L - LEFT) → PAM8403 RIN
Should be:    J511 Pin 5 (HPO_R - RIGHT) → PAM8403 RIN

That's it! Just ONE wire, ONE pin over to the left.


QUICK REFERENCE
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

J511 Header (official Jetson Nano documentation):

Row A (top):      1    3    5    7    9
Row B (bottom):   2    4    6    8   10

Key pins:
  Pin 2 = AGND (Ground) ✓ Already correct
  Pin 5 = HPO_R (RIGHT audio) ← MOVE WIRE HERE
  Pin 9 = HPO_L (LEFT audio) ← Currently here (WRONG)


THE FIX (5 minutes)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Step 1: Unsolder from Pin 9 (2 minutes)
  1. Heat soldering iron
  2. Position on solder joint
  3. Wait 2-3 seconds
  4. Pull wire away
  5. Let cool

Step 2: Solder to Pin 5 (2 minutes)
  1. Position wire on Pin 5 (one position left)
  2. Heat soldering iron
  3. Touch iron to wire and pin
  4. Add pea-sized solder
  5. Let cool - should be shiny!

Step 3: Test (1 minute)
  cd /home/severin/dev/r2d2
  python3 test_audio_fixed.py
  → You should HEAR the beep now!


WHY THIS WORKS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

PAM8403 has:
  • LIN (Left Input) → L+ and L− outputs → Left speaker
  • RIN (Right Input) → R+ and R− outputs → Right speaker (YOUR SPEAKER)

You connected your speaker to the RIGHT outputs (R+/R−), so you need the
RIGHT audio input (Pin 5), not the LEFT audio input (Pin 9).

Simple matching rule:
  LEFT channel → LEFT outputs
  RIGHT channel → RIGHT outputs

Your setup:
  RIGHT audio source (Pin 5) → RIGHT outputs (R+/R−) ✓ CORRECT (after fix)
